Homeland Security, Immigration & The Environment

I believe we secure our nation not through walls and fear, but through smart policy, strong institutions, and stewardship of the land we inherited.

Immigration

Full amnesty and a pathway to citizenship, rebuilding the asylum system, and humane enforcement reserved for violent offenders.

DHS Reform

Abolishing ICE, dismantling DHS, and restoring FEMA, Secret Service, Coast Guard, and CISA to independent agencies.

National Defense

Air and naval dominance, Pentagon audits, Coast Guard funding, VA reform, and a seamless military-to-civilian transition.

Environment & Public Lands

The Scout Law of Conservation, a Civilian Conservation Corps 2.0, Paris Climate Accords, and net-zero by 2050.

Immigration & The Border

I believe in full amnesty and a pathway to citizenship.

I advocate for the regularization of the millions of undocumented immigrants currently living, working, and raising families in the United States. I reject the fantasy of mass deportation, which would be a moral and economic catastrophe. Instead, I support a streamlined, efficient pathway to citizenship for those without violent criminal records. By bringing these millions out of the shadows, we strengthen our economy, secure our tax base, and uphold our values.

I view immigrants as an economic engine, not a burden.

I recognize the overwhelming data showing that immigrants drive massive economic activity, start businesses at higher rates than native-born citizens, and are essential to our labor force. I believe that a simpler, quicker, and more accessible legal immigration system is the only way to truly secure the border. By removing the “negative incentive” of a broken system, we incentivize legal entry and allow law enforcement to focus on actual threats.

I support a targeted, humane enforcement strategy.

I believe Title 8 enforcement should be strictly reserved for violent offenders. I fundamentally oppose immigration enforcement actions in protected “sensitive locations” such as churches, hospitals, and schools. Using these essential services as traps for deportation is a violation of public trust and public safety.

I believe the asylum system must be rebuilt, not abandoned.

The right to seek asylum is codified in both US law and international treaty, and I reject any attempt to eliminate or effectively nullify that right through bureaucratic attrition. However, I also recognize that the current asylum system is broken—not because too many people are seeking protection, but because the infrastructure to process their claims has been deliberately starved of resources. There are currently millions of cases backlogged in immigration courts, with wait times stretching years.

I advocate for a massive expansion of immigration court capacity.

I support the immediate hiring and appointment of hundreds of additional immigration judges, the establishment of dedicated asylum processing centers at and near the border, and guaranteed access to legal counsel for all asylum seekers—including unaccompanied minors. The fastest way to end the perception of a “border crisis” is not to build walls, but to build courtrooms. When claims are adjudicated quickly and fairly, those with legitimate fears of persecution are protected, those without valid claims are promptly and humanely returned, and the system commands the public trust it currently lacks.

I reject “Safe Third Country” agreements that export our obligations.

I oppose policies that force asylum seekers to apply for protection in countries they transited through—countries that often lack the legal infrastructure, safety conditions, or political will to protect vulnerable populations. The United States should not outsource its moral and legal obligations to nations that cannot meet them. We process our own claims, under our own law, with our own resources. That is what a serious country does.

Department of Homeland Security Reform

I call for the abolition of ICE.

I advocate for the complete dismantling of Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). I believe this agency, born in the panic of the post-9/11 era, has become a rogue paramilitary force that threatens civil liberties and terrorizes communities. Its history of human rights abuses, warrantless surveillance of American citizens, and lack of accountability proves it is beyond reform.

I propose returning immigration functions to their original agencies.

I believe we must return to a pre-9/11 framework where immigration services are managed by the Department of Justice (ensuring due process) and customs enforcement is managed by the Department of the Treasury (focusing on trade and commerce). We must treat immigration as a legal and economic matter, not a permanent national security emergency.

I demand the radical restructuring of the Department of Homeland Security.

I view the DHS as a failed experiment in government centralization. I believe there is no logical reason for FEMA, the Coast Guard, CISA, and the Secret Service to exist under the same bloated umbrella. This consolidation has led to inefficiency, mission creep, and a dangerous lack of oversight.

I advocate for the independence of essential agencies.

  • FEMA: I believe FEMA must be restored as an independent, cabinet-level agency focused solely on disaster response, free from the politicization of aid distribution.
  • Secret Service: I support returning the Secret Service to the Department of the Treasury to focus on its original dual mandate of protection and financial crime investigation.
  • Coast Guard: I advocate for returning the Coast Guard to the Department of Transportation during peacetime, ensuring it receives the specific support it needs as a maritime service rather than getting lost in a massive security bureaucracy.
  • CISA: I believe cybersecurity is too critical to be buried in DHS; it should operate as an independent agency akin to the EPA, focused entirely on digital infrastructure resilience.

National Defense & Military Strategy

I believe in “Peace Through Strength” via global reach.

I unequivocally support the continued funding and development of the United States military. I believe that a strong America is the greatest deterrent to global chaos. Specifically, I advocate for the prioritization of the US Air Force and US Navy. In an era of Pacific competition, our ability to project power and protect freedom of navigation relies on superior air and naval dominance, not on permanent land armies. These branches are the primary instruments of our global reach and must remain technologically unrivaled.

I demand a full audit of the Pentagon and Defense Contractors.

I believe that being pro-military means being anti-waste. I support ongoing, rigorous, and independent audits of the Department of Defense. I condemn the culture of price-gouging where defense contractors overcharge the American taxpayer for basic supplies. Every dollar wasted on fraud or inefficiency is a dollar not spent on the safety of our service members. I believe in a lean, lethal, and accountable military industrial base.

I view the US Coast Guard as a pillar of Homeland Security.

I advocate for the full funding and support of the US Coast Guard. As the first line of defense in our coastal waters and the preeminent search and rescue force in the world, they are often the most overstretched and underfunded branch. I believe in equipping them with the modern cutters and aircraft necessary to stop drug interdiction, protect our maritime borders, and save lives at sea.

Our obligation to those who serve does not end at discharge.

A nation that asks its citizens to risk their lives in uniform and then abandons them to bureaucratic neglect upon return has broken its most sacred promise. I view the Department of Veterans Affairs not as a bloated entitlement program, but as the living embodiment of a debt this country owes. I reject any proposal to privatize or dismantle the VA system; I believe in funding it, reforming it, and holding it accountable.

I demand the elimination of the VA claims backlog.

I support massive investment in VA staffing, technology modernization, and claims processing infrastructure to eliminate the unconscionable delays that leave veterans waiting months or years for disability determinations and healthcare access. I advocate for the expansion of VA mental health services—particularly for PTSD, traumatic brain injury, and military sexual trauma—and for the integration of veteran suicide prevention into every level of VA care. We lose more veterans to suicide each year than we have lost in combat in most of our recent wars. That is a national emergency, and I refuse to treat it as a statistic.

I believe in a seamless military-to-civilian transition.

I support a federally coordinated transition program that ensures every separating service member has access to employment counseling, skills certification and credential translation, housing assistance, and continued healthcare coverage during the transition period. I advocate for the expansion of veteran hiring preferences in federal contracting and for tax incentives to private employers who hire and retain veterans. The skills forged in military service—leadership, discipline, technical expertise, grace under pressure—are exactly what our economy needs. We must build a bridge, not a cliff, between service and civilian life.

Environment & Public Lands

I believe in the “Scout Law” of Conservation.

I am an Eagle Scout. I did not learn about the environment from a textbook; I learned it in the humid scrub of the Ocala National Forest and the sawgrass of the Everglades. I was raised on a simple, powerful rule: “Leave No Trace.” I believe this rule should apply to our nation. We have a moral obligation to leave this country better than we found it. I reject the false choice between economic growth and environmental stewardship. We must manage our natural resources not as infinite commodities, but as a national trust.

Our Public Lands are the training ground for citizenship.

Growing up hours from some of the most unique ecosystems on Earth, I learned that our public lands are the great equalizer. In the woods, your bank account doesn’t matter—your character does. I view our National Parks and Forests as the physical manifestation of democracy. I advocate for initiatives that get every American student into a National Park before they graduate, using our lands to foster civic responsibility. When you sweat to maintain a trail or sleep under the stars, you develop a stake in your country that no classroom can teach.

I advocate for a “Civilian Conservation Corps 2.0.”

My time in Scouting taught me that service builds strength. I support the revitalization of a modern Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC) to address the maintenance backlog in our parks and harden our infrastructure against climate change. This program would offer young Americans a chance to serve their country, learn trade skills, and rebuild the physical and spiritual health of the nation through hard work in the outdoors—just as I did in Central Florida.

I am a “Florida Realist” on Climate Change.

I don’t need a report to tell me the climate is changing; I have watched the water tables rise and the storms intensify in my own backyard. I view environmental protection not as a partisan issue, but as a survival strategy. Protecting the Everglades isn’t just about saving birds; it’s about protecting the drinking water for millions of Floridians. I support the Paris Climate Accords and strict environmental protections because I know that if we destroy our natural defenses, no amount of money will save us from the consequences.

I support a net-zero emissions target by 2050 with binding interim benchmarks.

I believe the United States must commit to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions no later than 2050, with a binding interim target of reducing emissions by at least 50% below 2005 levels by 2035. These are not aspirational slogans—they must be codified into law with enforceable sector-by-sector reduction plans, annual progress reporting, and accountability mechanisms that survive changes in administration. I support aggressive investment in carbon capture and sequestration technology, reforestation programs, and the rapid electrification of transportation. The science is clear, the economics increasingly favor the transition, and the cost of inaction—measured in superstorms, droughts, crop failures, and climate refugees—dwarfs the cost of investment. As a Floridian, I do not have the luxury of treating this as an abstract debate. The water is rising. The time for half-measures has passed.
